Character Keeper

Keeper Principles
  • Play to find out.
  • Make the world realistic.
  • Be a fan of the Mavens.
  • Sometimes disclaim decision making.
  • Keep it cozy.
  • Make nighttime dangerous.
  • Keep dangerous characters occluded. ('Stalker in the Shadows')
  • Present the Void in a matter-of-fact way.

    • Keeper Reactions (Go hard!)
  • Inflict a condition (Encourage players to come up with appropriate conditions.)
  • Separate them (or force them together)
  • Threaten or endanger a PC or NPC.
  • Kill a suspect
  • Kill a Maven (Promote Move: 'Putting on a Crown')
  • Have a Cozy Place item lost, stolen, broken, etc.
  • Have an official show up
  • "Cut to commercial!" (Once per session, change a failed roll into a success.)

    • Keeper Tips
  • Frame Scenes like a TV show or movie. (Pan, Zoom, Action, Cut, Flash-back, Commerical Break, etc.)
  • Ask the players what their characters are thinking and feeling, and what they want to do.
  • Encourage rolepaying. Encourage adding details.
  • Fiction -> Move -> Fiction.
  • Remind players to use their 'Cozy Place' items.
  • Point to items which can be added to the 'Cozy Place'.
  • Utilize clocks and set plans in motion.

    • Conditions
  • Conditions are usually negative traits imposing disadvantage on dice rolls.
  • A character can have up to three conditions.
  • Instead of a 4th condition, make the move 'Putting on a Crown'.
  • Conditions are removed through 'The Cozy Move' or at the Keeper's discretion.

    Putting on a Crown

    When text from those sections is marked during play, the player of the Maven in question must do as the text instructs. This frequently means narrating a very specific type of scene. Players can narrate such scenes whenever they wish, but must do so before the End of Session

    The Crown of the Queen (Choose and mark one.)

    The Crown of the Queen represents the idea of your Maven as a woman. The text involves flashbacks to her life as a partner, mother, and sister; and present-day scenes that show her being romantic or sexual, or having a complicated inner life. The entries can be marked in any order.

    • A flashback of your fondest memory of your late partner.
    • A flashback showing how you were an imperfect sister or daughter.
    • A flashback showing how you were an imperfect mother.
    • A flashback of your fondest memory with one of your children.
    • A scene in the present day showing a private side of you very few get to see.
    • A scene in the present day showing a burgeoning romance.
    • A scene in the present day showing how you satisfy your physical desires.
    The Crown of the Void (Mark the first empty box)

    The Crown of the Void represents the effects of the dark conspiracy on your Maven. The text is always marked in order, and the last box heralds your Maven's retirement from the game.

    • A Shadow in the Garden. Hereafter, during cozy vignettes focused on you or Cozy Move scenes involving you, you must also narrate how dark entities subtly reveal themselves in the scene.
    • The Chariot. Your Reason modifier is reduced by 1 and your Sensitivity modifier is increased by 1.
    • Pallid Mask. Hereafter, during any intimate conversation with another character, you must make a casual reference to death, dying, the afterlife, or the End of All Things‚Äîno matter the subject at-hand.
    • The Pomegranate Kernel. You take the Condition: Obsessed with the Void. It can never be cleared.
    • The Void. Retire your character in a way that shows how they are lost to the Void.

    Pitch the Game
  • The game is inspired by TV detective shows and cozy crime dramas.
  • You are group of elderly women.
  • You belong to the 'Murder Mavens Mystery Book Club'.
  • The 'Murder Mavens Mystery Book Club' meets every Saturday evening at 'The Candlelight Booksellers'.
  • The 'Murder Mavens' are particularly inspired by a fictional detective called: Amanda Delacourt.
  • You are an amateur detective. You have already helped the local police solve several high profile crimes, however the local authorities are not always comfortable with your meddling and nosing around!
  • Sometimes, people ask you for help with trouble. Sometimes, trouble finds you!
  • Sometimes people speculate that you are committing the crimes in copycat infatuation with crime writers!
  • You live in Brindlewood Bay which is a small, coastal community in Massachusetts.
  • Brindlewood Bay is a time-capsual town turned tourist-spot, with antique shops, dining spots, fishing trips, and sometimes B-List celebrity sightings.
  • You enjoy your golden years by pursuing hobbies, and finding comfort and companionship in each other.

  • Mark two 'End of Session Questions' (Max 3).

    • Introduce a New Mystery?
  • A maximum of 3 active mysteries is recommended.
  • The Keeper may choose to present a new mystery.
  • Present the mystery 'Title' and 'Complexity'.
  • Use 'Presenting the Mystery' as a prompt for framing one or two scenes in which the mystery is presented to the Mavens.
  • Ask the 'Establishing Question'.
  • Begin the mystery!
    • Session End
  • The session ends when the mystery is solved or real time runs out.
  • If 'real time' runs out, aim to end the session on a cliffhanger.
  • Players refer to their 'End of Session Questions'.
  • For each marked question, a 'Yes' answer equals 1 XP.
  • Mark XP earned on the 'XP Track'.
  • When all five XP Track boxes are marked, erase them and gain an 'Advancement'.
  • When all 'Advancement options are marked, the character can continue to play, but no longer advance.
